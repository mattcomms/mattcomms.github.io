Case Study (hyperthetical) Podist

Client: Butler Development

Task: UI design of a new podcast app

Role: UX/UI designer

Duration: 12 weeks

Year: 2021

Butler Development is a small start-up business founded by James Butler. James and his team are tech-savvy developers that have a passion for podcasts. They are looking to develop their own Podcast app called ‘Podist’ (name subject to change). As developers, they have a strong understanding of the importance of the UX process to ensure they are getting user-driven results in the hope to stand out from the other applications out there on the iOs market.

The purpose is to create a podcast app on the iOS platform that encourages user engagement beyond a podcast player. Many podcast apps such as the stand-out leaders, ‘Apple Podcast’ and 'Spotify' don’t give the user much to do outside of listening to episodes. The application is targeting users that want to do more than just listen, the idea is to introduce a new way to interact with podcasts.

1.4 Competitor Analysis

Audit / review Apple podcasts app

Overview

The Apple podcasts app definitely tackles complexity in a simple way. There is a lot going on behind the scenes on this app that is not realised until you look deeper into how it is pieced together.

For the general use this app is more than enough to listen and follow along with podcasts.

One of the core issues found from this audit/review was an issue with regards to ‘Rating & Review’. In some cases this area in question is impacted by unplayed episodes. This area is not the only section that is impacted by this issue, the same goes for ‘Hosts and Guests’. A feature that could clearly be expanded upon and used in a more interactive way.

Ratings could possibly play a bigger role in the browse section of the app. Highlighting its popularity and its quality.

Audit / review Spotify app

Overview

It is important to note that Spotify was first and foremost a music playing app. Podcasts appear to have been added to the platform afterwards and as a direct result have been missing some core functionality that other podcast apps utalise such as Reviews. Podcasts can be found tucked away under the Search area of the app.

Upon survey results it is clear people use Spotify to listen to music as well as listen to podcasts as the app covers more than one area of entertainment. Spotify’s strong music audience plays a key part in obtaining podcast listeners.

The Queue functionality seems to be hidden deep within levels that most users would overlook.

2.2 Empathy mapping

I collated all the important information from the research to form the following Affinity map. I was able to narrow down the infromation into User behaviour, Features and Frustrations. From here I was able to group areas further by similarity: Conversation, saving, sharing and detailed information.

Pains

  • Sharing issues: instructions to listen to a specific part of an ep - too time-consuming and friends don't act on it
  • Facebook groups can get flooded with memes and other such items that don’t relate to the episodes
  • Users have trouble finding the information they find useful and want to come back to
  • Apple podcasts currently link bigger podcasts hosts and guest to other shows/episodes they feature on but give no information about them.

Gains

  • The ability to time stamp sections of a podcast to reference later and save comments alongside it
  • The ability to have conversations with purpose and a clear indication as to why the conversation is happening
  • An ability to find out more about the people they find interesting enough to listen to
  • Easily send content to friends in bite-size format to know they aren't wasting their time sending friends content that won't be consumed because 'it was just too hard'.
